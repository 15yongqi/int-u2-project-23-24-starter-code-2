Tian Tian and Meixiang return to China

Mei Xiang and Tian Tian arrived in the United States in 2000. The two newly arrived giant pandas were first leased to the United States for ten years according to a formal agreement. The two parties later signed two extension agreements, each for five years, and the last one was five years. The one-year extension ended at the beginning of the COVID-19 outbreak. The Chinese partners of the agreement agreed to an extension for another three years, and the family returned to China in November this year.

Panda Mei Xiang was born in 1998 at the Hetaoping Rewilding Training Base of the China Giant Panda Conservation and Research Center. Arriving in the United States in 2000.

Giant panda Tiantian is a male giant panda born in Wolong National Nature Reserve. He moved to the National Zoo in the United States on December 6, 2000 and will live there until November 2023.

Little Miracle is a male giant panda who was born and now lives at the National Zoo. Meixiang and Tiantian were conceived through artificial insemination on March 22, 2020, ranking fourth among their surviving children. But he also returned to China in November this year.
